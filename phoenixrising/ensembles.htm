PHOENIX RISING:
ENSEMBLES AND DIRECTORS


• All Saints' Women's Chant Choir
Scott Youngs, Director
    The Women's Chant Choir of All Saints' Episcopal Church was formed in 1996. This choir sings the bulk of the Evensong services at All Saints', along with Eucharistic services, concerts, and special offerings throughout the community. They currently have three CDs, one in conjunction with Musica Dolce, an early music ensemble. Their repertoire varies from chant to contemporary setting of texts to support worship. Their pure unison sound creates a restful and contemplative atmosphere for services and ties us to the ancient sounds of the early Church.

• Central United Methodist Sanctuary Choir
Donald E. Morse, Director
    The Sanctuary Choir of Central United Methodist Church has performed throughout Eastern and Western Europe, Greece, the Americas, the Caribbean and the South Pacific. They have sung at the invitations of the Archbishops of Milan, Salzburg, Florence, Siena, Perugia, Sorrento, Venice, Berlin, Dresden, Prague, Hradec Kralove, Santander, Santiago de Compostela, Toledo, and Sevilla, presenting concerts in each of those cathedrals, and have performed in the great basilicas across Europe. The Choir was honored to have been sponsored by the Pontificio Instituto di Musica Sacra and Amici Della Musica Sacra in Italy presenting a series of concerts in Rome, including the Mass of the Angels at the Basilica Church of St. Peter at Vatican City, and a private devotional concert in the Sistine Chapel. The Choir was pleased to have been selected as one of four American Church Choirs to participate in the first Salzburg Church Music Festival to highlight American Sacred Music, in the summer of 1987. They are well known for their versatility in performing literature from many periods, particularly music of the twentieth century. In addition to providing music for the regular worship services of Central Church, they present major works with chamber orchestra, Broadway musicals, yearly community concerts, and have appeared on Central Church's weekly television program. They have performed on numerous occasions with The Phoenix Symphony and have performed for festivals, both regional and national conventions of the American Choral Directors Association, the Music Educators National Conference, the American Guild of Organists, and United Methodist Denominational gatherings. A volunteer choir, they are comprised of professionals in many career areas whose common interest is serious music making in sacred settings.

• Valley Chamber Chorale
Christopher Samuel, Director
   Building on a nucleus of singers who were graduates of the Paradise Valley High School Concert Choir, the Chorale invited Christopher Samuel in 1988 to become conductor and artistic director, and expanded into a community choir that included members from all parts of the Valley. Ultimately reorganizing as an auditioned chamber choir under a not-for-profit status, the group eventually took the name Valley Chamber Chorale in order to reflect the breadth of the area communities represented by its members. Since 1988, the ensemble has performed at the state convention of the Music Educators National Conference in 1991 and 1995, the American Guild of Organists Region IX Convention, Summer Soundsplash '95 (in Phoenix), and the World's Largest Organ Concert with guest artist, Joan Lippincott, in 1996. The group's compact disc of Renaissance motets and madrigals, O Sing Joyfully, was released in 1996, and can be found in bookstores locally and in select cities nationally.

• Bronzeworks Handbell Choir
Kay Cook, Director
   Bronzeworks™ is a performance/touring group comprised of teen ringers, ages twelve to nineteen. These ringers have advanced from Junior Bronzeworks, or successfully auditioned for acceptance. Bronzeworks ringers have developed the advanced musical ringing skills required to execute highly complex rhythms and musical arrangements for stage and theater performances. Members must be relatively flexible with regard to their schedule and be able to travel throughout the United States and internationally when the group is booked for performance or recording engagements. There are approximately ten to fifteen performances and/or recording commitments each year in addition to two to four out of town engagements each year. International tours are usually booked in the summer every two years. Bronzeworks is well known throughout the world for their accurately synchronized musical ringing, technical dexterity, and creative interpretation of musical compositions and their delightfully entertaining style and showmanship on stage. They have been featured many times throughout the years on all the local Arizona television stations. Most recently, Channel 12 and Sean McLaughlin, featured Bronzeworks during the second week of competition at the 2002 Winter Olympics, as they performed at various venues throughout Park City and the Olympic Village and on the nationally syndicated Today Show. In December of 2000, Bronzeworks performed in the production of Christmas Pudding at the Celebrity Theater, in conjunction with several national celebrities. Bronzeworks introduces newly published works to the musical community. Their recording, Bronzeworks ‘95, features a variety of original compositions and some familiar favorites like Boogie Woogie Bugle Boy and Battle Hymn of the Republic. Red River Publishing recently announced they were planning to publish a series of CDs dedicated to the performances of Bronzeworks. Performing tours have included the United States, Canada, Great Britain, Japan, Belgium, France, Germany, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Czech Republic, Austria, Hungary, and Australia.

• The Sonoran Brass Quintet
Joshua Whitehouse, trumpet; Jason Johnson, trumpet; Nathan Mitchell, horn; Richard Bass, trombone; Chuck Kerrigan, tuba;
   Acclaimed as an ensemble of the highest caliber, the Sonoran Brass Quintet has been captivating audiences for more than a decade. The summer of 2008 saw the much-anticipated release of the Sonoran Brass Quintet's first CD, entitled Dry Heat. The Sonoran Brass Quintet has been featured with the Mesa Community College Band performing Eric Ewazen's Shadowcatcher, a concerto for brass quintet and wind ensemble. The ensemble was the only brass quintet invited to present a recital as Guest Artists at the 2007 Southwest Regional Tuba/Euphonium Conference in Tucson, Arizona. The members of the quintet are also participants in the Arizona Music Project, a new program by the Arizona Office of Tourism designed to feature some of Arizona's most talented musicians. The Sonoran Brass Quintet has collaborated in concert with the Phoenix Children's Chorus and Orpheus Male Chorus of Phoenix and will be joining the Grammy-winning Phoenix Chorale (formerly known as the Phoenix Bach Choir) for their December concert series. They are Artists in Residence at Central United Methodist Church in Phoenix, where they are featured regularly in services and recitals. The quintet is also active in the arrangement, commission, and performance of new works. All of the members of the quintet are avid supporters of music education and teach privately or are adjunct faculty at colleges in the Phoenix area. The members of the Sonoran Brass Quintet are Jupiter Performing Artists.
